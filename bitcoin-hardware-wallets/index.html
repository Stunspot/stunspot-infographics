RISK ¬∑ LIFECYCLE & FAILURE

Math Lasts. Electronics Don‚Äôt.

The cryptography inside these devices ages gracefully. The chemistry and mechanics around it do not. Custody planning means designing around the parts that rot.

Li-Ion Batteries
Risk: swelling, capacity loss, storage sensitivity Window: ~3‚Äì5 years under typical use

Internal Li-ion is the stealth attack surface for ‚Äúcold storage‚Äù if the device lives in a drawer or safe. Plan refresh cycles, or use AAA / no-battery designs for anything you expect to just sit and wait for years.

Screens & Touch Panels
Risk: dead pixels, ghost touches, total failure

Beautiful screens make for better onboarding, but they add new ways to die. Ghost touches + signing is a nightmare scenario. Keep at least one ‚Äúdumb input‚Äù signer in any serious multi-sig setup.

Firmware & Protocol Drift
Risk: obsolete formats, stagnation, quiet deprecations

Wallets that stop getting active engineering become harder to operate safely over time. Favor devices with clear update cadence and release notes, and do an annual ‚Äústill fits our stack?‚Äù review.

Production & Supply Shocks
Risk: early-batch quirks, surprise EOL

First runs surface weirdness (screens, plastics, assembly). Later, SKUs vanish. The sweet spot: slightly after launch, once issues are known, and before sunset ‚Äî with a small buffer of spares you trust.
